We plan to create "Carbon Clicker", a browser-based greenwashing awareness game with a twist! The goal is to build the wealthiest company on Earth while hiding your catastrophic environmental impact from the public. The intention is to create a satrical "idle game" akin to [Cookie Clicker](https://orteil.dashnet.org/cookieclicker/) where you slowly amass wealth but also increase your pollution and must balance a public perception meter. If you reach too low a perception you will lose, so you must mitigate this by buying "Greenwashing Upgrades" which are *suspiciously similar* to some real world events. E.g., ["Invent a new sustainability phrase to absolve you of responsibility"](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2021/aug/23/big-oil-coined-carbon-footprints-to-blame-us-for-their-greed-keep-them-on-the-hook), ["Rig your machines to cheat on emissions tests"](https://www.bbc.com/news/business-34324772) or even ["Fund Academics to lie about climate change"](https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2015/feb/21/climate-change-denier-willie-soon-funded-energy-industry).

The goal of this project is to provide an engaging educational environment - something a lot of similar ideas tend to fall short of unfortunately, where users will learn not only about existing Greenwashing scandals, they will be provided the tools to spot such occurrences much easier in the future. The air of dark humour combined with the role reversal provides a  memorable perspective on the issue; a unique opportunity to explore the topic of greenwashing in an interesting way. This game leverages that by making greenwashing tactics fun to exploit, which paradoxically teaches why they're harmful. It is also really accessible to Software Engineers as it's an ideal toy to leave in a browser tab and play with every few hours while on break or in a gap between work or meetings. 

We plan to create the website in TS/React and have it accessible via GitHub Pages as a proof-of-concept.
